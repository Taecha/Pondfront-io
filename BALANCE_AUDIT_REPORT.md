# PondFront.io Balance Audit Report

## Scope

Audited animals, abilities, committed combat, economy, bots, maps, objectives, alliances/support, and mobile combat usability. This pass focused on small balance changes rather than adding new features.

## What Was Too Strong

- Frog was the clearest outlier in the 30-match baseline simulation, winning 12/30 matches. Its lily income, Lily Farm synergy, objective priority, and Big Leap cluster size stacked too well.
- Long-map building totals were high: Large matches averaged 219.8 buildings built. Economy was not instantly broken, but farm spam had too much long-term efficiency.
- Carp income was healthy and sometimes high. Carp was not dominant in the 30-match baseline, but post-patch sample variance showed it should remain on the watch list.

## What Was Too Weak

- Turtle won only 2/30 baseline matches. It defended well in local fights, but its slow expansion and low attack tempo made it underperform on Medium/Large maps.
- Current Push had 0 uses in the 30-match baseline. This is not overpowered, but it was too invisible in bot play.

## What Was Confusing

- Combat is much clearer after the committed-wave rework, but players still need the UI to reinforce that Bite/Push/Wave commit energy once and then stop automatically.
- Defense is understandable in the tile panel, but reinforced borders can still look similar to naturally strong terrain unless border status is enabled.

## What Felt Fair

- Duck stayed close to target: 5/30 wins, good beginner expansion, not best late-game fighter.
- Snake stayed close to target: 5/30 wins, strong attacks without obvious economy dominance.
- Max attacks are powerful but risky because they spend all energy immediately.
- Defense is meaningful without fully erasing attack pressure.

## Buffs Needed

- Turtle needed a small tempo buff, not a huge defense buff.
- Bots needed slightly more Current Push visibility, especially Normal/Hard/Chaos.

## Nerfs Needed

- Frog needed a small mobility/economy nerf.
- Lily Farm scaling needed a mild anti-spam adjustment.
- Carp economy needed a light tap down, without removing its identity as the economy animal.

## Systems Kept Unchanged

- Duck Flock Rush stayed unchanged.
- Snake Ambush stayed unchanged because Snake win rate was fair.
- Core combat wave rules stayed mostly unchanged after the committed-wave pass.
- Defense caps stayed unchanged to avoid making Turtle impossible to kill.
